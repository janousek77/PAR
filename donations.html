Help Save Lives!

Making a donation to any Seattle area animal shelter is one of the simplest ways you can save a life and prevent pet homelessness. Want to help but don't have or want to give money? Most shelters need supplies, volunteers, or people to foster animals. Here is a list of local shelters in the Seattle area. Click on any picture and it will take you directly to their site.

Emerald City Pet Rescue exists to rescue abused, neglected and homeless animals. We pull predominantly from high-kill shelters around the country and place them into loving forever homes. Sometimes these animals come from abroad and sometimes from right around the corner. Emerald City Pet Rescue is run by dedicated staff and volunteers who give their time and resources to house, train, rehabilitate, transport and care for these animals.

Forgotten Dogs Rescue is a foster home based rescue organization located in Washington State. We are dedicated to rescuing homeless and abandoned dogs that are at risk of euthanasia in shelters, given up by their owners because of difficult circumstances, or in danger of abuse or neglect.

The Seattle Animal Shelter Foundation was formed by a group of volunteers to help the animals cared for by the shelter by providing unrestricted operating funds to advance the shelter's mission. The Foundation is focused on enhancing and expanding the programs of the shelter beyond their current boundaries.

Thank you for your generosity towards helping the animals in our care. There are several ways you can donate!

Located in Woodinville, Homeward Pet finds new homes for over 1800 cats, dogs, puppies and kittens every year. We are dedicated to providing excellent care to our animals, and great service to our two-legged visitors.

With respect and compassion for all animal life, MEOW promotes lifelong relationships between people and companion animals, providing shelter and care for each precious life until adopted into a forever home.

Motley Zoo Animal Rescue is dedicated to improving the lives of animals locally, nationally and internationally; alleviating their suffering and elevating their status in society through the rescue and rehabilitation of animals in need and through the education of their community on responsible pet ownership and animal welfare, advocating the value of all animal life, in hopes to end the neglect and abuse of all animals and to end the devastating pet overpopulation through spay and neuter advocacy.

The Auburn Valley Humane Society enriches the lives of companion animals and people through animal sheltering, programs, services and community engagement
